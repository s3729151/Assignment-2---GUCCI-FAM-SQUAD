Project Idea

One project I have in mind is developing software that will help authors like me with writing, outlining, and laying out our books for print and e-book format. This will be a lot like the currently available platform named Vellum, but hopefully cheaper and easier to use. The software will have features that will be of use to authors who want to do their own formatting, as currently the costs of layout and book editing are quite high. I also hope to have something that will help the target market with their need to interact with beta readers and write without distractions. In this project, there will also be a feature where the author will be able to directly upload it on e-book retailers such as Amazon.

Currently, there is a huge market of readers both locally and internationally, who are shifting from physical books to retailers. As a result, the line between traditional publishing and self-publishing for authors are continuously getting blurred. This project aims to help new authors such as myself to reach their market without having to shell out huge amounts of money, which authors could instead invest in marketing. The presently available software, Vellum, is quite expensive. Sure, in Australia, it is rather affordable, but back in the Philippines - the place I call my home, the average salary is much lower, therefore making the price of the software more prohibitive for those who are still starting out.

The project will give the author access to a number of features, the main one being the ability to lay out their book in print or e-book format, depending on their needs. In the interface, the customer can change their book's metadata, edit the appearance, add decorations or clip-art stuff pasted on the page for the readers to distinguish between chapters or sections. The customer will also be able to decide on the overall look of their book. For beginners, it will have a wizard to guide them through the works and the basics. Outlining and plotting services / features will also be built in, to help authors with the writing aspect of things. Handy tools such as word count both at the chapter and book level will be available for the author's monitoring. In this software, I hope to incorporate a feature where more than one user will be able to access. Authors usually have beta readers who provide a fresh take or perspective on the manuscript. Using the software, I want to have a feature for beta readers where they will be able to comment on the book's current look.

As the customers get used to the software, there will be features available for them to customize their book's look, and saved templates will also be a possibility, so that authors will be able to put their own unique touch on all of their books and future releases, similar to how the Harry Potter and Percy Jackson books are all formatted in the same way. Ornamental features will also be at the customer's disposal, and they will be able to input images, and subheads for the book. Trim sizes and format options will be available as well once the layout is finished, and users will be able to preview what their book will look like once printed on paper or on Kindle, as well as other book platforms or channels such as Smashwords and Gumroad. The software, in addition, will have features to pick up stuff such as page breaks and headings to help it break the text apart in chapters automatically. I also plan to incorporate in the project a way for the author to format book series or boxed sets, which usually help in promotions. This project, unlike the currently available software in the market, will be made available for both Mac and PC users, making it accessible to all kinds of authors who wish to do their own book layouts. There will also be options for the user to add color in the books if they wish. The book cover will also be checked by the software if the size or resolution is just right for the selected measurements.

The software, once rolled out, will be available for purchase on a website for PC users, then on the App store for Mac users. Software needed will be similar to features of the basic word processor. As for hardware needs, the project will simply have to be on a PC or a Mac, nothing too fancy. As for codes and features, I'm still going to learn this along the way, hopefully. No other equipment will be needed.

As there are no special hardware needed for this project, the only skills that will be needed are for coding. Some special programming know-how will have to be employed to be able to design a word processor with so many add-ons. There will also be a need to reach out to Amazon and other e-book reading platforms so as to have access to uploading manuscripts from the software to their database. While this skill level and permissions required may take some time to be acquired, this project, in my opinion, is very much feasible as long as the developer or team handling it is committed.

For the outcome, I expect the software to be successful with the target market, as it incorporates everything that every starting author needs, from outlining, to writing, and to finally publishing their work out in the international market. I expect the outcome to have more authors having access to channels they didn't think was possible. With the roll-out of this software, I hope to empower authors, especially from third world countries such as mine. This way the authors will not have to depend on traditional publishers to get their books out. Hopefully, with the use of my project, authors such as myself could reach new boundaries and readership bases. With affordable software that caters to their writing and publishing needs, the original problem of having prohibitive prices to publishing software and high publishing costs will be addressed.
