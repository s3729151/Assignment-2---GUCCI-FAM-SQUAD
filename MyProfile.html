Project Idea

Split is a mobile application for the iOS (which can be implemented into the Android too) which allows its users to transfer money without the hassle of entering details such as their BSB, branch number etc. where a singular mistake can lose you money. With Split, the user just has to create an account with their banking details, or PayPal account and their basic information. After that, they’ll be ready to use the app. All users have to do to transfer money; is to enter the amount they want, hold a virtual button and then “tap on” phone to phone.

Sure, transferring money’s relatively easy, however transferring methods such as bank transfer takes way too long when it comes to transferring money across different branches, for example moving money from Bank of Melbourne to ANZ can take 3-7 business days. The users also have to fill in details (such as BSB, account number, branch number etc.) of the person who they’re transferring to, which is another hassle – making a simple mistake can cost you all that money that you were trying to transfer, which is also the same problem with moving money across in PayPal.

In detail, Split serves as a digital money transfer application. When first time launching the application, the user has to create a secured account. They sign up with their basic information such as name, address, phone number etc. as well as their credit card/debit card information such as their card number and security code. The users also have the option to use PayPal instead which is quicker and safer. After signing up, the users can log in – in which they’ll be greeted by a feature where the user can “add” their Facebook friends on their Split friends list. If they don’t have a Facebook account, the phone number will be used as a way to add people to Split’s own friends list.

The friends list function allows the users to transfer money with ease. They can just go to their friends list - tap on their name, enter an amount you want to transfer, confirm the transaction and it’s done! The users also have the ability to choose where the money goes, either into their PayPal account or straight into their bank account.

Now the main reason as to why Split is unique in comparison to the other money transferring apps, is that users can “tap” with their friends to transfer money. What this means is that the users can bump their iPhones with their friend(s) (Bluetooth or airdrop has to be on in order to work) in order to transfer money easily. This resolve’s in having to manually transfer money over to your friend during an outing such as eating out in dinner. This makes splitting money a lot more convenient and less awkward. The instructions on doing so are relatively straight forward and simple. The user opens the application and the first page shown to them will have an integer box where the user can enter an amount of money to transfer/split. After entering an amount, they are greeted with a confirmation screen. If the user agrees, the app will showcase a small cancel button and a “Split” button. The user has to hold the split button and then bump their phone with the other user – who is in receiving mode (they have to hold a digital button as well). After bumping, the money will process and transfer with ease.

Split can be used in a plethora of ways, for example splitting money after a dinner a large group in order to save the hassle of paying individually at the cashier – wasting your time and the cashier’s time too! Users can split money on the spot via tapping with friends or transferring money by using the friends list function. The possibilities are endless when it comes to transferring with Split!

In order to create split I would have to use PayPal’s API/developing kit, same with banking. I’d also need to take into massive consideration the security of the application, making sure I have the best software to stop user’s information from being exploited (number 1 priority!).

As for the software programming, the language I would use is Swift since it’s tailored towards the iOS device – which means that I’ll be using XCode to create the application. The hardware that I’ll be creating the application with would have to be a MacBook or an apple computer since – again, the program is tailored to apple devices.

As for the skill, I would have to learn how to use XCode, and learn the Swift language in order to create the best UI for the user. I would also need to research the best security when it comes to the user’s important information. Another important skill to have in creating this app is the implementation of PayPal and bank accounts into the Split – as to which I’m completely clueless with as of now. Programming the application and implementing the payment methods will be hard to do, however there are a lot of resources online!

If Split is successful, people won’t have to have the tedious and inconvenience of transferring money through bank transfer or manually entering details every time. It also saves time transferring between different branches such as ANZ to Bank of Melbourne – which can take up to 7 business days to transfer. Split also allows users to transfer money on the go, by tapping on which saves the inconvenience of having to pay separately at the counter. Split just makes money transferring easy and satisfying (when tapping with a friend or friends).
